About the Collection

The Origins of Value collection comprises over 868 historical financial documents spanning four centuries and 54 countries. Assembled at the International Center for Finance at the Yale School of Management, it traces the evolution of financial instruments — from the earliest known shares of the Dutch East India Company to twentieth-century securities issued across Europe, Asia, and the Americas.

Each document in the collection tells a story of commerce, innovation, and risk. Together, they form a remarkable record of how societies have organized capital, financed exploration, and weathered financial crises from the South Sea Bubble to the modern era.
