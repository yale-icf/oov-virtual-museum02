# Transcription

THO' I have already taken the Trouble and Pains to fettle the following Lottery Scheme of four Pound each Ticket, that they may have a Choice either for the prefent or the future yet I conceive the fame will be a Detriment to many People ; whereas my publifhed Guinea Lottery Scheme delivered laft Week, can hurt none, but will pleafe every body ; and though there are nine Blanks to a Prize, thofe who will take four Tickets will make it hard only to one and half Blanks a Prize ; befides, that to have one Ticket of four Pound, or four at one Guinea each, is four Chances to one ; and with four Guineas they have the Chance to 4000l. and be a prudent Pattern to fhew the Probability of my publifhed great Propofal for this Nations Welfare and Happinefs, which I have much at heart, &c.

SCHEME

100,000 Tickets (either more or lefs in equal Proportion) at four Pound each Ticket to depofit one Pound, and one Pound every Month afterwards, is 400,000l.

Thofe 100,000 Tickets to be drawn by quadruple Numbers, with quadruple Prizes, fo that 25000 Numbers will determine the whole Lottery, and fave Time, Trouble and Expence.

4 Prizes of     10000 each, is     40000
8               5000              40000
40              1000              40000

800             50                40000

12000           10                120000

15272 Prizes                amounts to £ 394000
84728 Blanks (is only five and half Blanks to a Prize)

100000 Tickets

For the firft Drawn at 500l. 2000
For the laft Drawn at 1000l. 4000

Amounts to £ 400000

Note, From the Prizes are to be deducted 12 per Cent. for the intended Purpofe, and to be paid either in Money, or in Annuities of 3 per Cent.

My Propofal for leffening the national Debt is real Good, and my Intention for it is ftill better, and if the Parliament and Government are pleafed for the Sake of their Community to encourage me with their prudent public Approbation, I will find out other great Matters for the Glory, Honour, and Welfare of his excellent Majefty and this Kingdom, and which is my chief Intention, moft earneft Defire, and humbly pray for it, &c.

London, April 1753.                JACOB HENRIQUES.
