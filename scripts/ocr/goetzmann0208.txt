But whatever momentary relief or aid the minister and his bank might expect from this low coining five pound notes, it will encrease the inability of the bank to pay the higher notes, and halfen the destruction of all; for even the small notes that used to be paid in money will now be paid in those notes, and the bank will soon find itself with scarcely any other money than what hair powder guineas tax brings in.

The bank notes make the most serious part of the bundles of finance; what is called the national funded debt is but a trifle when put in comparison with it; yet the cafe of the bank notes has never been touched upon. But it certainly ought to be known upon what authority, whether that of the minister or of the directors, and upon what foundation, such immense quantities are issued. I have heard the amount of them at fixty millions sterling; I have produced data for that estimation; and besides this, the apparent quantity of them, far beyond that of gold and silver in the nation, corroborates therewith. But were there but a third part of fixty millions, the bank cannot pay half a crown in the pound; for no new supply of money, as before said, can arrive the bank, as all the taxes will be paid in paper.
