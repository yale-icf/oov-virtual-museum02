**Page 332 - Anno Regni octavo Georgii Regis.**

Inhabitants of the Islands of Nevis and Saint Christophers, who sustained great Losses by the Invasion and Depredation of the French, to encourage them to resettle in the said Islands; and in another Act made in the Tenth Year of Her late Majestick Reign, taking Notice that the Bounty aforesaid would exceed by amount at least to One third part of the said Losses sustained by the Planters and Inhabitants of the said Islands only, it was Enacted, that Her Majesties Commissioners for the Affairs of Trade and Plantations, and the One or more of them, or such Proof as was herein prescribed, should or might forthwith sift out, in the respective Claims of every such Sufferers, his, her, or their Errors or Admixtures. Notwithstanding, for One full third part of their respective Losses, specified in the Return of a Commission upon respective Requisites being first performed by the said Sufferers, their Agents or Assigns, as by the said Acts were to be done and performed) and that such Substitutes should be delivered out to them, of respective Agents or Attornies, and should carry Interest for the Principal Sums therein mentioned, after the Rate of Five and twentieth Day of December, One thousand seven hundred and fifteen; and there, as the Commissioners of Trade and Plantations did make forth Debentures, pursuant to the said Acts, which amount in Principal twenty to the Sum of twenty nine thousand three hundred fifty one Pounds, Sixteen Shillings, and the more, the Interest whereof, at the Rate aforesaid, hath been lasted until the Feast of the Birth of our Lord Christ, One thousand seven hundred and twenty one; and the Principal Sum, together with every one thousand seven hundred thirty one Pounds, Nineteen Shillings, and One Peny Farthing, to still remain unsatisfy'd: Now to the End the several Proprietors of the said Debentures may have a just and reasonable Satisfaction for the Principal and Interest due of unpaid to them respectively, It is hereby Provided by Authority aforesaid; That the said respective Proprietors, and their respective Executors, Administrators and Assigns, shall by virtue of this our Act be entitled to an Annuity after the Rate of Three Pounds per Centum per Annum, for and in lieu of the Principal and Interest due of unpaid to them respectively, &c.
