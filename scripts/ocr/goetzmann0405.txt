**English Translation:**

WE, the Protectors, Deputies, & Administrators of the New Monte Sussidio Vacabile of the City of Florence, established by order of the Most Serene Grand Duke of Tuscany our Lord, by Provision obtained in the Most Ample Senate of [the administrators]

To All those who shall see the present [document], we make clear and indubitable faith, as

[handwritten text - partially illegible]

appears [as] Creditor in the Book of said New Monte Sussidio, marked E, at [the value] of two hundred seventy ducats in Florentine money for every place, with the fruits of his benefit at the rate of six similar ducats per hundred per Year for every place from [date - illegible] going forward,

as by our decree of this day at [the office of] Permute, &c., signed C, at [location - illegible]—However, we, admitting the said Creditor of said Monte in said places for place, as above, payable in three-month intervals in three installments per payment

[handwritten text - illegible]

and with the privileges, prerogatives, conditions, and declarations which in said Provision, and in our Decree of amplification and declaration of the 8th of August 1692, to which all shall always refer: Ordering by virtue of that to our Chancellor the present [certificate], which shall be signed by one of Us Protectors, and countersigned by our Chancellor and sealed with the folio seal of said Monte.

Given in Florence in our usual Residence on [date - illegible]
