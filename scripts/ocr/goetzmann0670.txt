## English Translation:

**[Top Left]:** 125 RUB. GOLD
6th Issue.

**[Top Right]:** 500 FR.
6th Emission.

**[Header Banner]:** IMPERIAL RUSSIAN GOVERNMENT.

**[Main Title]:** RUSSIAN FOUR PERCENT GOLD LOAN SIXTH ISSUE OF 1894,

FOR ONE HUNDRED THIRTEEN MILLION SIX HUNDRED THOUSAND RUBLES IN GOLD = 454,400,000 francs = 367,155,200 German marks = 17,971,520 pounds sterling = 217,203,200 guilders = 87,472,000 gold dollars,

ISSUED ON THE BASIS OF THE HIGHEST IMPERIAL DECREE OF FEBRUARY 9, 1894

in the quantity of 328975 bonds of 125 rubles gold, or bonds in foreign currency according to article No. 3125 p.p. Nos. 857076 and 2069 sheets with twenty-five bonds (in 3125 p.p. Nos. 857076 to No. 858980).

for repayment of outstanding shares, dependent on officials and clerks of the Main Society of Russian Railways.

**A. BOND** 125 Rup. Gold
No. 277166

IN ONE HUNDRED TWENTY-FIVE RUBLES IN GOLD
= 500 francs = 404 German marks = 19 pounds sterling 15 shillings 6 pence = 239 guilders = 96.25 gold dollars.

**TO BEARER.**

The holder of this bond has the right to the sum of ONE HUNDRED TWENTY-FIVE RUBLES IN GOLD.

Four percent per annum, accruing in full to the bondholder four times per year: April 1, July 1, October 1, and January 1 annually.

**IN ST. PETERSBURG:** in the STATE BANK, and other designated locations at the course rate;

**IN LONDON:** in the Russian Agency for Baring Brothers & Co.; in German marks;

**IN PARIS:** in the Russo-Foreign Bank, as well as Credit, National Discount Office, and General Railways

**IN BERLIN:** and T. Discount, Roxuntany and Co.; in German marks;

**IN AMSTERDAM:** and T. Bruns, Magnus and Co.; in dollars.
