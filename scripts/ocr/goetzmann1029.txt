# Transcription

**Original (Dutch):**

O. 1198

De Directeurs van de generale Keyserlyche Indische compagnie, ordonneren aen hunnen cassier Joan Baptist Cogels junior, te ontfangen van D.M. Joseph Bergkin in Wernen de Somme van twee hondert en vijftigh guldens wissel gelt, voor het eerste payement Syndercactie vaneenduijsent guldens in het Capitael vande Selve compagnie, op de conditien in het Octroy breeder Vermelt, Stellende quitantie hier onder, actum in Antwerpen derthien Augusti Seventhien hondert drij en twintiagh

Ontfangenvan D.Heer Jodeph Bergkin de Somme van twee hondert vijftigh guldens Wissel gelt voor het eerste paijement adje 8. October 1723

Solvit als boven het 2de kayement 8. December 1723

Solvit als boven d'Helft van het 3de Bayement 13 decemb. 1724

Solvit als boven onder de helft van het Derde Rayement 2. Novemb. 1725

**English translation:**

The Directors of the General Imperial Indian Company order their cashier Joan Baptist Cogels junior to receive from D.M. Joseph Bergkin in Wernen the sum of two hundred and fifty guilden in exchange money, for the first payment of one thousand guilden in the Capital of the same company, under the conditions more broadly set forth in the Charter, granting receipt hereof, executed in Antwerp the thirteenth of August seventeen hundred and twenty-three.

Received from D. Mr. Joseph Bergkin the sum of two hundred fifty guilden in exchange money for the first payment on 8 October 1723.

He paid as above the 2nd payment 8 December 1723.

He paid as above the half of the 3rd payment 13 December 1724.

He paid as above the remaining half of the third payment 2 November 1725.

**Notable markings:**

- Coat of arms (double-headed eagle with Imperial insignia) at top center
- Reference number "O. 1198" (upper left)
- Multiple signatures in cursive script (right margin):
  - Corveyaard
  - Jacomo de Poli
  - Peroletti
  - B. Bogells Lame (appears multiple times on payment endorsements)
- Handwritten payment endorsements with dates: October 8, 1723; December 8, 1723; December 13, 1724; November 2, 1725
- Aged, cream-colored parchment or paper with visible wear and foxing
