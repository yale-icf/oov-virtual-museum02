# Transcription

**Title:** Filth Pool

**Text at bottom:**

How famous is the Man, that could contrive,
To serve this Gluttonous Sonn with Fish alive?
But now we're bubbl'd by his Fishing Pools,—
And as the Men catch Fish the Fish catch Fools.—

**Notable markings:**

- Four spade symbols visible in the upper left corner (suit indicator for playing card)
- Engraved illustration in classical style showing a large merchant vessel with multiple masts and sails, accompanied by smaller boats. Large fish and sea creatures are depicted in the water below, with sailors engaged in fishing activities.
- This is a satirical playing card from the South Sea Bubble era, using the "Filth Pool" as metaphorical commentary on financial speculation and fraud.
