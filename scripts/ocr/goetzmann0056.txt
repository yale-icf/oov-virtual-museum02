# Transcription

**Title (top right):**
Building Ships to left to freight

**Suit indicator (top left):**
Seven of Hearts [displayed as seven red hearts arranged in a standard playing card pattern]

**Main text (bottom of card):**
Who but a Nest of Block-heads to their cost,
Would build new ships for freight, when trade is lost?
To raise fresh Barks, must surely be amusing,
When Hundred rot in Docks, for want of using.

---

## Notable markings:

This is a satirical playing card from the South Sea Bubble series, using verse to critique the economic speculation and poor business decisions of the era. The engraving style is typical of early 18th-century British financial satire.
