# Transcription of French Consular Certificate of Egyptian Financial Document, Alexandria, 1866

**Original (French):**

Bon sur le ministère des finances.

En vertu de l'arrêté du ministre de l'intérieur en date de [unclear] 1865 correspondant au [unclear] 1281, le ministre de l'intérieur a ordonné à l'Imprimerie de l'État, à Alexandrie, la confection de 300 imprimés contenant les formules à remplir de leur ordre, en ministère des finances.

Ait seulement de formule ayant reçu l'ordre impérial au d'imprimer ledit Drogman d'apparat et Dames.

Du 23 juillet 1799.

Le ministère de finances

P.D. mahmoud Raïs Pacha.

Pour Ratification, certifié aux, Alexandrie le 12 Mai 1866.

Le Drogman [illegible]

B[illegible]

Vu pour légalisation de la signature ci Bassou N.B D. [Affermier Bonne?] Drogman, le Consul Général de France à Alexandrie, d'appui.

Alexandrie, le 12 Mai 1866.

L'Agent du Conseil Général
Le Consul

---

**English translation:**

Bond on the Ministry of Finances.

By virtue of the decree of the Minister of the Interior dated [unclear] 1865 corresponding to [unclear] 1281, the Minister of the Interior ordered the State Printing Office in Alexandria to produce 300 printed forms containing the formulas to be completed at their order, by the Ministry of Finances.

Only had received the imperial order to print said Dragoman of apparatus and Ladies.

From 23 July 1799.

The Ministry of Finances

P.D. Mahmoud Raïs Pasha.

For Ratification, certified in Alexandria on 12 May 1866.

The Dragoman [illegible]

B[illegible]

Seen for legalization of signature herein Bass N.B D. [Farmer Good?] Dragoman, the Consul General of France in Alexandria, for approval.

Alexandria, 12 May 1866.

The Agent of the General Council
The Consul

---

**Notable markings:**

- Two official seals of the French Consulate General at Alexandria (circular stamps with French imperial eagle and text)
- Multiple handwritten signatures
- Date: 12 Mai 1866 (12 May 1866)
- Reference to 195,000 Piastres in the document context
- Notarial attestations by French Consular officials
