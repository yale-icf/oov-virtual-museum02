# Transcription

**Original (Dutch):**

Zie hoc de Zeevd, en West, en Missi;
sippr zweven
Op eene schop, wyl zy 't van lugt
en winden leven,

**English translation:**

See how the South, and West, and Miss[issippi winds]
together float
Upon a spade, while they from air
and winds sustain their boat,

**Notable markings:**

- Number "3" in top right corner (indicating this is the Three of Spades)
- Engraved illustration in woodcut style typical of 17th-century Dutch playing cards
- Text appears at bottom of card in period Dutch script
- The card depicts three female allegorical figures representing wind directions, seated on what appears to be a spade, with one holding a flower, another holding an ornate staff, and the third holding a large teardrop or spade-shaped symbol
- A small cherub or cupid figure appears at top left
