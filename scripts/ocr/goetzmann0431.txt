## Notable Markings:

- **Serial Number:** No. 055,199
- **Denomination stamps:** 4½% interest rate clearly marked
- **Royal Seal/Crest:** Circular medallion in center featuring heraldic imagery (double-headed eagle or similar)
- **Historical stamp:** Small blue/teal colored stamp in upper left corner (appears to be an Austrian or Bulgarian royal stamp)
- **Red text elements:** "ПРЕДОСТАВЯЕТ'" and other key terms highlighted in red in Bulgarian section
- **Ornamental borders:** Extensive decorative filigree throughout document in beige/tan tones
- **Printer attribution:** Waldheim-Eysenschmidt & Co., Vienna (bottom)
- **Bearer bond status:** Clearly marked as "ОБЛИГАЦИЯ/OBLIGATION" (bearer security)

## English Translation of Key Sections:

**Title:**
KINGDOM OF BULGARIA. REALM OF BULGARIA.

Bulgarian State 4½% amortizable loan in gold from 1909 / State Loan of Bulgaria 4½% Gold 1909

**Loan Details:**
Created by virtue of the law of November 27, 1909 and sanctioned by Royal Decree of December 7-13, 1909, ratified by the contract concluded between the Royal Government of Bulgaria and the Wiener Bank-Verein of Vienna.

**Represented by 200,000 Obligations**
with total capital of 1,000,000,000 gold francs
(95,200,000 Austrian crowns = 81,000,000 German marks = 19,160 British pounds sterling = 9.65 Dutch guilders)

**Redeemable at par over 50 years.**

**Denomination:**
Five hundred gold leva = 476 Austrian crowns = 405 German marks = 19.16 British pounds = 9.65 Dutch guilders

**Interest:** 4½ percent annually payable June 1st and December 1st of each year.

**First coupon date:** June 1, 1910 (new style calendar)
