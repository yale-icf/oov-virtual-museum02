# Transcription

**Title (top of card):**
Temple Mills

**Caption (bottom of card):**
By these Old Mills, Strange Wonders have been done
Numbers have Suffer'd, yet they still Work on;
Then tell us which have done the Greater Ill,
the Temple Lawyers, or the Temple Mill.

**Notable markings:**
- Portrait of a playing card figure (King of Diamonds) in upper left corner, wearing ornate period clothing with a red diamond symbol
- Engraved illustration showing Temple Mills with brick buildings, a water wheel on the right, figures in 18th-century dress in the foreground, and architectural structures in the background
- This is identified as part of a set of South Sea Bubble satirical playing cards from Great Britain
