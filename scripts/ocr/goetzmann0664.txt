# Transcription: Bank of England Transfer Receipt

**INDIA £3 PER CENT. STOCK.**

**RECEIVED** this 5 Day of June 1889 of [signature] The Proprietors to protect themselves from FRAUD, are recommended to ACCEPT by themselves or their Attorneys all TRANS-FERS made to them.

Transfer days:
- Monday,
- Tuesday,
- Wednesday,
- Thursday,
- Friday.

Holidays excepted.

(2640)

[Provided a Bank of England Wider Loan Representation]

Fred Bonner Flindell Esq. & Catherine Mary Tapp (wife of William Ellicombe Tapp, formerly Catherine Mary Flindell) hereinafter called the said Transferee Flindell,

the sum of

being the Consideration for

Interest or Share in the Capital £3 PER CENT. STOCK, created under Act of Parliament enabling the Secretary of State in Council of India to raise Money in the United Kingdom for the Service of the Government of India, transferable at the Bank of England, together with the Proportional Annuity attending the same, by this day transferred to the said Transferee

Witness my Hand

[Signature]

Witness

[Signature]

- 6d Contract Note stamp (bottom right)
- Handwritten calculations on right side: £423.0.6, brokerage 10.7, stamps 3.6, total £423.11.7
- Multiple signatures (witness and transferor)
- Date stamp marking "24/6/89" (top right corner)
- Document reference number (2640) at bottom left
