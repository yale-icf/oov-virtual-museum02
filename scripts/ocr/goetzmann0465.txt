**Original (French):**

TONTINE ROYALE Aoüst 1734. 2.e Classe.

M. De Raphanneuf
Subdivision

PARDEVANT les Contrôllers du Roy, Notaires au Chastelet de Paris, souffignez, furent presens Messire MICHEL ESTIENNE TURGOT, Chevalier, Seigneur de Souffons, Bons, Uffy, Potigny, Perciers, Brucourt, & autres lieux, Conseiller du Roy en ses Conseils, President au Parlement & en la seconde Chambre des Requestes du Palais, Prevost des Marchands; Claude-François Petit, Ecuyer, Conseiller du Roy, Quartiniers; Jean-Baptiste de Santeul, Ecuyer; Jean-Baptiste Tripart, Ecuyer, Conseiller du Roy & de la Ville; & François Touvenot, Ecuyer, Conseiller du Roy, Notaire au Chastelet, tous Eichevins de cette Ville.

Lesquels ont dit, que le Roy par son Edit du mois d'Aoüst 1734. registré où besoin a esté, auroit, pour les causes y contenués, ordonne qu'il seroit par les Commissaires de Sa Majesté nommeroît, vendu & alienné quatorze cens soixante-trois mille livres actuelles & effectives de rente viagere, sous le titre de Tontine, fusette à accroissement, distribuée en quinze classes, composées de differentes divisions, dont chaque action feroit de trois cens livres de capital; lesdigtes rentes à prendre sur le produit des droits d'Aydes & Gabelles, & des cinq grosses fermes, que Sa Majesté à [speciellement] & par privilege, affectez, obligez & hypothequez au payement & continuation des arrerages desdites rentes, même par préference à la partie du trésor Royal, dont les constitutions seroient faites à ceux qui defereroient les acquerois sous leurs noms, où autres qu'ils nommerioent, avec faculté à chaque personne de prendre tel nombre de parties de rente qu'il lui plairoit dans chaque subdivision de la classe, pour lesquelles il lui seroit expedié des contrats: Que de la rente de chaque rentier qui viendroit à deceder, un quart des arrearges de fadite rente seroit estenu à la defcharge des revenus de Sa Majesté, & les trois autres quarts appartiendieroît par accroissement aux survivans de la subdivision de la classe, entre lesquels ils seroient distribuée d'année en année jusqu'au dernier mourant; en forte que le dernier vivant de chaque subdivision de chacune desdites classes, recueilleeroit feul les trois quarts du revenu des capitaux qui composeroient ladite subdivision, & que lesdites rentes pourroient auffi estre acquises par les estrangers non naturalizez, même ceux demeùrans hors du Royaume; à l'efect de quoy Sa Majesté auroit renoncé à tous droits d'aubaine, confiscations, & autres qui pourroient lui appartenir: pour, des arrearges desdites rentes, seûoit par les acquéreurs leur vie durant, comme de leur propre chose, vray & loyal acquelt, en vertu des contracts qui seroient faits par lesdits Sieurs Brevolt des Marchands & Eichevins, & estre payé des arrearges desdites rentes à bureau ouvert par chacune année, en deux paiemens, sur lesquittances en parchemin, paffez devant Notaires en la forme portée audit Edit, à commencer du premier Octobre 1734. pour ceux qui auroient fourni leurs fonds avant le premier Janvier 1735. fans que lesdites rentes puiffent estre fujettes à aucunes faites pour quelque caufe que ce soit, même pour les dettes & affaires de Sa Majesté, ni estre reduites ni retranchées fous quelque pretexte que ce puiffe estre: en outre, que les rentes qui seroient acquises par les estrangers, seroient exemptes de toutes lettres de marque & de represaille, ainfi qu'il eft plus au long porté audit Edit; en execution duquel a esté faite vente & alienation par Messieurs les Commiffaires du Conseil, Procureurs speciaux de Sa Majesté auxdits Sieurs Brevolt des Marchands & Eichevins, desdites quatorze cens foixante-trois mille livres de rente, par contract paffé en vertu des Lettres patentes y enoncées, pardevant Bourron & ion conferere

# English Translation:

ROYAL TONTINE August 1734. Second Class.

M. De Raphanneuf
Subdivision

BEFORE the Controllers of the King, Notaries of the Châtelet of Paris, undersigned, were present Messire MICHEL ESTIENNE TURGOT, Knight, Lord of Souffons, Bons, Uffy, Potigny, Perciers, Brucourt, and other places, Counselor of the King in his Councils, President of Parliament and in the Second Chamber of Petitions of the Palace, Provost of the Merchants; Claude-François Petit, Esquire, Counselor of the King, Quarternier; Jean-Baptiste de Santeul, Esquire; Jean-Baptiste Tripart, Esquire, Counselor of the King and of the City; and François Touvenot, Esquire, Counselor of the King, Notary of the Châtelet, all Aldermen of this City.

Who declared that the King, by his Edict of the month of August 1734, registered where necessary, would have, for the reasons contained therein, ordered that by the Commissioners of His Majesty appointed, fourteen thousand sixty-three thousand livres of actual and effective life annuities would be sold and alienated, under the title of Tontine, subject to increase, distributed into fifteen classes, composed of different divisions, each share consisting of three hundred livres of capital; said annuities to be charged on the proceeds of the rights of Aides and Gabelles, and the five major farms, which His Majesty had specially and by privilege, affected, obliged, and mortgaged to the payment and continuation of the arrears of said annuities, even in preference to the portion of the Royal Treasury, the constitutions of which would be made to those who would purchase the shares under their names or others whom they would name, with the faculty for each person to take such number of annuity shares as they pleased in each subdivision of the class, for which they would be issued contracts: That from the annuity of each annuitant who should come to die, one quarter of the arrears of said annuity would be retained for the discharge of the revenues of His Majesty, and the three other quarters would belong by accretion to the survivors of the subdivision of the class, among whom they would be distributed year by year until the last survivor; so that the last living member of each subdivision of each of said classes would collect alone the three quarters of the revenue of the capital which would compose said subdivision, and that said annuities could also be acquired by foreigners not naturalized, even those residing outside the Kingdom; for which purpose His Majesty would have renounced all rights of escheat, confiscations, and others which might belong to him: for, from the arrears of said annuities, would be paid by the acquirers for their lifetime, as of their own property, true and loyal ownership, by virtue of the contracts which would be made by said Messieurs Brevolt of the Merchants and Aldermen, and be paid of the arrears of said annuities at open bureau by each year, in two payments, upon receipts on parchment, passed before Notaries in the form contained in said Edict, commencing from the first October 1734 for those who would have provided their funds before the first January 1735, provided that said annuities cannot be subject to any proceedings for any cause whatsoever, even for the debts and affairs of His Majesty, nor be reduced or diminished under any pretext whatsoever: furthermore, that the annuities which would be acquired by foreigners would be exempt from all letters of marque and reprisal, as it is more fully contained in said
