# Transcription

**Anno Regni Octavo Georgii Regis. 327**

And for the more speedy raising of Money towards making good the Sinking Fund, as aforesaid, It is hereby further Enacted by the Authority aforesaid, That it shall and may be lawful to and for the Commissioners of the Treasury, or any Three or more of them now being, or the High Creatures, or any Three or more of the Commissioners of the Treasury for the time being to cause such Sum of Money to be raised upon such Callies of Pro or Misgument, or other Callies, or upon Annuities to be payable in either thereof, as aforesaid, by Mortgage, Sale, or otherwise, and at or to such Place or Offices, or at such Rate of Interest or for such Continuances, as to the said Commissioners of the Treasury, or High Creatures for the time being, shall seem most expedient, and thereupon to cause any Argument or Allignment of the said Callies and Annuities, or any of them, to be made to the Person or Persons, Body Politick or Corporate, who should purchase the said Callies, or any of them, or the said Annuities, or any of them, or shall lend any Monies upon the said Callies and Annuities, or any of them, as the said Commissioners of the Treasury, or High Creatures for the time being, shall judge to be reasonable; all which Annuities, in respect to the said Sum of One hundred and fifty thousand pounds, Seven Shillings, and Eleven Pence Three Farthings, shall nevertheless be subject to such Redemption by Parliament, as is by the said recited Act provided touching the ordersAnnuities, which have been or shall be purchased thereupon.

And it is hereby Enacted, That all Sums that shall be raised, as aforesaid, for or towards the said Sum of One hundred two thousand he hundred eighty five Pounds, Seven Shillings, and Eleven Pence Three Farthings, as shall be applied towards making good to the Sinking Fund, as aforesaid, any of which the said Exchequer Bills, which were sent to the South-Sea Company, as is above mentioned, are directed to be paid off and cancelled, as aforesaid: any other Law or Statute to the contrary notwithstanding.

Provided always, and it is hereby aforesaid, That all Sums lent, and to be lent to this Parliament, upon One sit of this Session of Parliament, [intitled, An Act for granting an Aid to His Majesty by a Land-Tax to be raised in Great Britain, for the Service of the Year One thousand seven hundred and twenty two;] and to such Money (if any held be) of the Ear thereto granted, as shall audit and remain after all the Loans made or to be made on to the Act last mentioned, by that Act or any other Act of this Session of Parliament, transferred or to be transferred to the Register for the said Land—
