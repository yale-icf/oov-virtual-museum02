# Transcription of Chinese Imperial Government 4½% Gold Loan of 1898 Bond Document

## LEFT PAGE

### Extracts from the Agreement referred to in the Bond.

The Imperial Government of China authorizes the Chinese Imperial Railway Company and the Deutsche-Asiatische Bank to raise a loan for the amount of Sixtyfive million Gold Yuan for the purposes and on the terms following:

[The text continues with detailed financial terms and conditions regarding the loan management, security, and administrative procedures. Key provisions include:]

- The loan shall be issued in Bonds of £25 Sterling each
- Bonds will be issued in London, Berlin and other financial centers
- Interest at 4½% per annum, payable half-yearly
- The Chinese Government is bound thereby
- Various conditions regarding the management of the loan, including provisions for amortization
- Clauses protecting the interests of bondholders
- Specification that charges on mortgage shall be raised or created except as authorized
- The bonds shall be a first charge on the revenues of the Chinese Imperial Railway Company
- Provisions for additional security through Customs duties
- Details about the service of coupons and bonds

[The text is dense with legal and financial terminology typical of 19th-century bond documentation]
