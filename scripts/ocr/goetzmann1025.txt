# Transcription: Da Ming Tong Xing Bao Chao (1 Guan Note)

## Original Text (Classical Chinese):

**Header (top, right to left):**
大明行钞一贯

**Central Denomination Panel:**
壹貫

**Lower Section (right to left, top to bottom):**
戶部奉
太祖高皇帝
制曰凡民間
買賣皆許用
寶鈔一貫準
銅錢一千文
除金銀外不
許別作踏樣
者被徒二年
仍沒其家産
[several characters unclear/illegible due to wear]
千[?]月[?]日

## English Translation:

**Header:**
Great Ming Circulating Treasure Note – One Guan

**Central Denomination:**
One Guan [worth 1,000 wen/cash]

**Lower Text (summarized):**
The Ministry of Revenue, following the mandate of the Great Ancestor Emperor [Hongwu Emperor], proclaims: All common people are permitted to use Treasure Notes in their buying and selling. One Guan note is equivalent to one thousand copper coins. Except for gold and silver, no other denominations or counterfeits are permitted. Those who violate this shall be punished with two years of forced labor and confiscation of their entire household property...
[remainder partially illegible]

## Notable Markings:

- **Red official seals** (official stamps) impressed on the note
- **Decorative dragon motifs** forming ornamental border frame
- **Woodblock printing** characteristic of Ming Dynasty currency
- **Significant wear and aging** with staining and fading throughout
- Text arranged in traditional Chinese vertical columns (right to left)
