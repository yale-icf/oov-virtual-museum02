# Transcription

**Figure 1 (Left):**
Come buy my young lambs for a trifle apiece,
And believe me, they're glad with a fine golden fleece;
Here are Two, and to keep all your spirits in tune,
There are Three Thousand more on the 14th of June.

**Figure 2:**
Tho' a dab, I'm not scaly—I like a good plaice,
And I hope that good luck will soon smile in my face;
On the 14th of June, when Prizes in *shoals*,
Will cheer up the *cockles* of all sorts of souls.

**Figure 3:**
My rabbits come buy,—but a rare bit I see,
Which the wealth of the Lottery Wheel may give me;
If fortune I catch, and I finger the cole,
By the powers! I'll then bid adieu to the pole.

**Figure 4:**
I hope very soon to get rid of my yoke,
For a Prize in the Lottery Wheel I've bespoke;
And I who knows how high in the world I may rise,
If Fortune should butter my bread with a Prize?

**Figure 5 (Right):**
For the gay fruits of nature what wish you can feel,
When compar'd with the *fruits* of the Lottery Wheel
My basket of fruit I'd exchange with great glee,
If one golden *pippin* they'd only give me.
