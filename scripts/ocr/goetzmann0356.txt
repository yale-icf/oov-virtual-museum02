# Transcription of Italian Government Bond Certificate

## Original Italian Text:

**Header:**
CERTIFICATO N° 001,300 | RENDITA di L. 20—

**Decorative border with ornamental design (blue)**

**Main Title:**
DEBITO PUBBLICO
DEL REGNO D'ITALIA

**Subtitle:**
Cinque percento
LEGGE 10 LUGLIO 1861

**Body Text:**
NEL GRAN LIBRO DEL DEBITO PUBBLICO DEL REGNO D'ITALIA s'iscritta
l'annua rendita di Lire [unclear] dovore della Cassa [unclear di Ministero] della Italico Interno.

Con godimento dal 1° Gennaio 18[62], le cui rate semestrali sono pagabili
il 1° gennaio e il 1° luglio d'ogni anno, mediante riscossione all'Esattore
del presente Certificato.

**Location and Date:**
FIRENZE, il [date unclear]

**Signatures and Officials:**
IL DIRETTORE GENERALE
[Signature] [Signature] [Signature]

**Registration Number:**
N° [number] del Registro di posizione.

---

## English Translation:

**CERTIFICATE N° 001,300 | ANNUAL INCOME of 20 Lire**

**ITALIAN KINGDOM'S PUBLIC DEBT
Five percent
LAW OF JULY 10, 1861**

In the GRAND BOOK OF THE PUBLIC DEBT OF THE KINGDOM OF ITALY is registered
the annual income of Lire [unclear] owed from the Treasury [unclear] of the Ministry of Finance.

With enjoyment from January 1st, 18[62], the semi-annual installments of which are payable
on January 1st and July 1st of each year, by collection from the Tax Collector
of the present Certificate.

FLORENCE, [date unclear]

THE DIRECTOR GENERAL
[Signatures]

---

## Notable Markings:

- **Portrait vignette:** King Victor Emmanuel II (center, oval bust frame)
- **Seals:** Two official circular seals of the Kingdom of Italy (upper left and lower left)
- **Decorative elements:** Ornamental blue borders with Renaissance-style scrollwork
- **Stamps and markings:** Multiple cancellation stamps, transfer stamps, and handwritten annotations throughout
- **Serial numbers:** 001,300 (top); additional registry numbers partially visible
- **Handwritten modifications:** Various signatures and administrative notations in ink
- **Text color:** Brown/sepia for printed text; blue for decorative borders
- **Condition:** Aged paper with visible wear, stains, and creasing
