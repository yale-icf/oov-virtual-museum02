# Transcription of Confederate States of America $1,000 Cotton Loan Bond (1863)

**CONFEDERATE STATES OF AMERICA**

**1000**

**LOAN AUTHORIZED BY ACT OF CONGRESS APPROVED APRIL 30, 1863.**

On the first day of June 1865, the Confederate States of America will pay to the Bearer of this BOND, at the Seat of Government, or at such place of deposit, as may be appointed by the Secretary of the Treasury, the Sum of ONE THOUSAND DOLLARS with interest thereon from the date hereof at seven per centum per annum, to be paid at the pleasure of the Government, at Richmond, or at the office of the Financial Agent of the Government in London at other places as the Secretary of the Treasury may direct. Coupons will be paid semi-annually at the places named above.

Interest Coupons attached hereto dated April 30, 1863, and numbered 1-4, or such supplementary [text unclear] to any Act of Congress approved April 30, 1863, entitled an "Act supplementary to an Act to provide for the funding and further issue of Treasury Notes."

In Witness whereof, the Registers of the Treasury in pursuance of the said Act of Congress hath hereunto set his hand and affixed the Seal of the Treasury at Richmond, this First day of June 1863.

[Signature] C.G. Memminger
Entered [illegible] N.M.
Recorded [illegible]

**REGISTER OF THE TREASURY**

---

**Bottom coupons (repeated four times):**

C.S. COTTON COUPON LOAN, APRIL 30, 1863.

The Confederate States of America will pay to bearer at New Orleans, Mobile, Savannah, Charleston, Wilmington, N.C. or Norfolk, SIXTY DOLLARS, for this year's interest due June 1, 1863, on a Bond for One Thousand Dollars of the Cotton Loan authorized by Act of Congress approved April 30, 1863.

---

**Notable markings:**
- Ornamental borders with wave patterns
- Portrait engraving (center top, likely a Confederate official)
- "1000" printed in four corners
- "ONE THOUSAND DOLLARS" on left panel
- Serial numbering areas (partially visible/illegible)
- Signature of C.G. Memminger, Secretary of the Treasury
- Four cotton coupon strips attached at bottom
- Official seals and decorative scrollwork throughout
