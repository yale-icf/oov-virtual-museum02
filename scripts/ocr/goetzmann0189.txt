# Transcription

**Left Page (Printed Text):**

Trustees, in Discharge of the First Fourteen half-yearly Payments; and so much of the said £50,000 "Three per Cent." shall be held by them, half-yearly, and paid to the Treasurers, as will pay the Sum of £3339 10s. the Sum that will be then due to the Annuitants.

In case all or either of the several Classes should not be completed by the First Day of *January* 1790, each Subscription shall be returned, with Interest after the Rate of Four *per Cent. per annum,* from the Time of Payment paid, unless such Class or Classes can be modified to the Satisfaction of the Parties concerned.

In case any Dividend shall remain unclaimed for the Space of Three Years, Notice shall be three Times inserted in the *London Gazette,* that such Dividend remains unclaimed; and unless the Parties entitled to receive the said Dividend shall claim the same within Three Months after such Notice, the said Dividend shall become forfeited, together with all further Claim to, or Interest in, the said Annuity; and the same shall be divided among, and continue to, the Survivors belonging to the Class wherein such Life was, after deducting the Expenses attending such Notice.

The 600,000 Dollars are on the same Security as the Loans made by the United States in *Holland,* which bear an Interest of Five *per Cent.* only; and the Capital Stock of those Loans now falls, on the Exchange at *Amsterdam,* at 94 *per Cent.*

If the Capital Stock of the United States should be paid off, the Sum being placed at Interest at Five *per Cent.* will produce nearly the annual Sum of £7000 Sterling.

Subscribers not completing their Second Payments on or before the First Day of *January* 1790, will forfeit their first Subscription.
