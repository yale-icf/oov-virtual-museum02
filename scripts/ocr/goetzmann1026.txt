# Ming Dynasty Banknote (Verso) Transcription

**Original (Classical Chinese):**

[Upper section - faded impression of front design, largely illegible due to verso printing]
大明通行寶鈔 [partially visible in faded impression]

[Large red official seal in center-lower portion - characters within seal are too degraded to read with certainty]

**English translation:**

"Great Ming Circulating Treasure Note"

**Notable markings:**

- **Large red official seal (chop/印章)**: Prominently positioned in the lower center of the verso. The seal appears to be a rectangular or square stamp typical of Ming dynasty currency authentication marks. The characters within are largely illegible due to image degradation and the fading inherent to this banknote's age.

- **Faded impression**: The verso shows a ghosted/reversed impression of the obverse (front) printing, which was common in banknotes of this era due to the printing and pressing process.

- **General condition**: Significant age-related deterioration with staining, spotting, and overall fading consistent with a 500+ year old document.
