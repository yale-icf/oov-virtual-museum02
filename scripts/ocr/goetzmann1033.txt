**English Translation:**

Constitution in Force
January 19, 1785

[HERALDIC SEALS]

BEFORE the Counselors of the King; Notaries, Keepers of Notes & Keepers of the Seal at the Châtelet of Paris, undersigned, appeared Etienne D'Hemeville Garnier [and others] 8.

The Indies, And Etienne le Cordier D'Olive Syndic of the Company of Directors of [unclear]

Directors of the aforesaid Company; which by virtue of the Order of the Council of State of the King of February 17, 1724, by which His Majesty granted to the aforesaid Company the Privilege & the liberation of December 13th of that year, taken in the General Assembly of Administration of the aforesaid Company, regarding the Lottery composed of Annuities [Rentes] payable, three in Cash, & the others in Life Annuities, which were to form a body of a Tontine of twenty-eight thousand [unclear] Ecu each, each numbered at Ecus, which Life Annuities were to be drawn annually & incessantly, which Annuities, thus charged upon the fund of the aforesaid Company, the said Sieurs Syndic & Directors could constitute an assignment for the benefit of the Subjects of His Majesty, as well as Foreigners, solely from Arrearages & increases thereof, must be established when even they might fall outside the Kingdom, & even in time of war, so that the said Annuities might be curtailed or stopped for whatever cause, not even for the proper needs of the affairs of His Majesty conformably to the Order of June 20, 1724, as it is more fully stated [...]

[Lower handwritten section discusses:]

M. M. Louis Despioch, Treasurer and [Court official] at the Bureau of Finance at the Generality of Montpellier, to hear by him [the terms] after [the decease?] and during the death of Antoinette Despioch the daughter who enjoys it after the decease of the Second [term?] Registered in Paris with Richelieu, furnished by Mr. Rochon, competent and accepting.

Nine hundred Twenty Livres

of Life Annuity; which Annuity the said Sieurs Syndic & Directors, appearing for & in the name of the aforesaid Company of the Indies, promise to pay & crown this [annuity] Jonnaites by each year, at an open Bureau, from the Funds of the aforesaid Company...

Under Receipts which can only be passed before Notaries in Paris [before] the same Notaries that shall have received the Contracts, which shall certify the life & existence of the Annuitants; And regarding the unfortunate domiciled in the City of Paris, as is practiced for the Life Annuities due by His Majesty: And this in two terms, the first [unclear]

M. Cappe No.
