## Body Text

To all whom it may Concern:

Be it known, That the Compañia Azucarera Baragua, a Corporation organized and existing under the laws of the Republic of Cuba, for value received, hereby acknowledges itself indebted and obligated to pay to the bearer hereof, or if registered to the registered holder hereof, the sum of **One Thousand Dollars** in gold coin of the United States of America, of the present standard of weight and fineness, on the **15th day of January, 1914**, with interest thereon from the date hereof at the rate of seven and one-half per centum per annum, payable semi-annually on the 15th days of January and July in each year, upon presentation and surrender of the annexed interest coupons at the office of the Trustee hereinafter named in the City of New York or at such other place as the Company may from time to time designate.

## Notable Markings

- **Serial Number:** No. M 728 (appears twice, top left and top right)
- **Cuban Fiscal Revenue Stamp:** Pink stamp affixed at lower left corner
- **Cancellation Mark:** Diagonal striped pattern overlaid across the entire document text
- **Ornamental Border:** Elaborate green decorative frame with scalloped edges
- **Central Vignette:** Engraving depicting sugar cane harvest scene with workers and oxen in tropical landscape
- **Decorative Elements:** Green ornamental designs in corners and circular seal at bottom center
