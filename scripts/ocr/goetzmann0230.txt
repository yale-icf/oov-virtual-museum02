# TRANSCRIPTION OF GREEK BOND CERTIFICATE, 1898

**Header (Greek):**
ΒΑΣΙΛΕΙΟΝ ΤΗΣ ΕΛΛΑΔΟΣ
(Kingdom of Greece)

**Title (Greek):**
ΔΑΝΕΙΟΝ ΕΙΣ ΧΡΥΣΟΝ ΗΓΓΥΗΜΕΝΟΝ 2 1/2 ο/ο 1898
Τῷ χάρει συγγράμματα τῆς Γαλλίας, Μεγάλης Βρεταννίας καί Ρωσσίας
ΟΜΟΛΟΓΙΑ ΑΝΩΝΥΜΟΥ 2,500 ΔΡΑΧΜΩΝ

**Left Border (French):**
EMPRUNT HELLÉNIQUE GARANTI 2 1/2 OR DE 1898

**Subtitle Information:**
No° 30,731 ½

**Central Section Labels:**

*French (left):*
ROYAUME DE GRÈCE
EMPRUNT OR GARANTI À 2½ 1/8 %

*English (center):*
KINGDOM OF GREECE
GUARANTEED 2½ /0 & FOR CENT
GOLD LOAN OF 1898

*Russian (right):*
ГРЕЧЕСКОЕ КОРОЛЕВСТВО

**Bond Details:**
Guaranteed: 2½-3,600 fr.
Bond For: £100

**Central Text Blocks:**

*French section (lower left):* 
[Lengthy paragraph describing the loan terms and conditions - partially legible]

Key phrases visible:

*English section (center lower):*
"The capital and interest of this loan are exempt for ever from every Greek tax."

"The interest is payable on the 1st April and 1st October of each year to the holders of the Coupons at the following places:

- In Amsterdam, at the Amsterdamsche Bank
- In Brussels, at the Banque Belgique
- In Bâle, at the Banque Belgique

The redemption of this Loan will be effected at par, by means of drawings, which will take place on the 1st January and 1st July in each year commencing on the 1st January 1901."

## English Translation:

**Header:**
Kingdom of Greece

**Title:**
Gold-Guaranteed Loan 2½% 1898
With the guarantee of France, Great Britain and Russia
BEARER BOND 2,500 DRACHMAS

**Bond Identification:**
No. 30,731 ½

**Central Figures/Labels:**
Kingdom of Greece
Guaranteed 2½% Gold Loan of 1898

**Key Terms:**
Guaranteed: 2½-3,600 fr.
Bond Value: £100

**Loan Description (from central English text):**
The capital and interest of this loan are exempt forever from every Greek tax.

Interest is payable on April 1st and October 1st each year at:
- Bank of England (London)
- Comptoir National d'Escompte and Société Générale (Paris)
- Disconto-Gesellschaft (Berlin)
- Amsterdamsche Bank (Amsterdam)
- Banque Belgique (Brussels)
- Banque Belgique (Basel)

Redemption will occur at par through drawings beginning January 1st, 1901, with drawings on January 1st and July 1st each year.

## Notable Markings:

1. **Red Wax Seal/Shield** (upper center): Octagonal design with "TEN SHILLINGS" marking (likely circulation marking)
2. **Greek Royal Coat of Arms** (upper center): Double-headed eagle with shield
3. **Classical Allegorical Figures**: Male warrior (left), female draped figure (right), centered male figure
4. **Ornamental Greek Key Border**: Throughout entire document
5. **Signatures**: 
   - Greek Minister of Finance (lower left)
   - Bank of England Chief Cashier (lower right)
   - Bank of England Authorized Cashier (lower right)
6. **Serial Number**: 30,731 ½
7. **Printer Mark**: "CH. CHABOT, IMP. DE PARIS" at bottom
