# Transcription

**Original (Dutch):**

Diogenes lantuarn heb ik bij dag
van noden,
't Vong de agtings Witte Valk die
and ren is ontyloden.

**English translation:**

Diogenes' lantern I have need of by day,
[when] the opinion of the White Falcon
and the rest is unloaded.

---

**Notable markings:**

- Card designation: Eight of spades (indicated in document context and visible number "8" repeated in the figure's clothing)
- Spade symbol visible in upper right corner
- This is page 20 of 55 from "Pasquins Windkaart" playing cards from "Het Groote Tafereel"
- Engraved illustration in classical style typical of 17th-century Dutch prints
- Figure depicted holding a bird (falcon) on one arm and a lantern on the other, standing next to a tall wooden structure
