# Transcription of Imperial Russian Three Per Cent Loan 1859 Bond

**IMPERIAL RUSSIAN THREE PER CENT LOAN 1859.**
**ONE HUNDRED POUNDS STERLING**

Inscription in the Great Book of the Public Debt of Russia representing a Capital of **ONE HUNDRED Pounds Sterling**.

Entered the _______ Book _______ folio 1546

The bearer of this Inscription is entitled to an annual Interest of **THREE per CENT**, to be paid half-yearly in London, One Pound ten shillings on the **1st OCTOBER** and **1st NOVEMBER** and One Pound ten shillings on the **1st MAY** and **1st JUNE** by Messrs **J. Thomson, T. Bonar & Co.**, or at the option of the holder in Berlin by **Mr. F. Mart, Magnus in Thalers** at the rate of six and three quarter Thalers per Pound Sterling on the said dates.

Attached to this Inscription are half-yearly dividend warrants up to **18 APRIL 1869**, with a talon. After the expiration of that date new dividend warrants will be delivered in exchange for the talon.

Extract from the Regulations of the **IMPERIAL COMMISSION OF THE SINKING FUND** (Code of Russian laws Tom. XI edit. 1857.)

§. 89. The payment of interminable annuities by the State, as well as in times of war as of peace, without any distinction whatsoever, whether the creditor be of a friendly or of a hostile nation.

§. 90. If a foreigner, proprietor of an Inscription, shall die, the Inscriptions shall pass to his heirs, in the order of succession established by the laws of the country, of which he was a subject.

§. 91. Investments in the irredeemable funded debt, constituting the inviolable property of the depositor are exempt from all imposts whatsoever.

Extract of the **IMPERIAL UKASE** of the **20 March 1859**, addressed to the Minister of Finance.

For the amortization of this Loan a special sinking fund, to be kept separate from the other Loans, will be assigned to begin from the year 1859, which fund will form annually 1½ % of the capital of the Loan. On the closure of this fund, for the purpose of buying in the inscriptions at the exchange of the day. If the holder of an inscription of this Loan should wish to have the same repurchased at any time at such price as he may require, the inscription to the bearer will be then changed into an inscription in the name of the holder, without dividend warrants, and the interest due on such inscriptions will be paid at the **IMPERIAL COMMISSION OF THE SINKING FUND** at **St. Petersburg** every half year, at the period fixed here above.

Countersigned: **THOMSON, BONAR & CO.**
per pro. **F. MART MAGNUS,**
Contractors.

Director of the Commission [**signature - illegible**]
