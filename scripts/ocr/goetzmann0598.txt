## MAIN TITLE

**THE CHINESE GOVERNMENT**

FIVE PER CENT REORGANISATION GOLD LOAN OF 1913.

FOR £25,000,000 STERLING.
£25,000,000 STERLING or MARKS 511,250,000 or FRANCS 631,250,000
or ROUBLES 236,750,000 or YEN 244,900,000.

**SINGLE OBLIGATION.**
Frs.505. = £20. or 409 MARKS. or 189.40 ROUBLES. or 193.82 YENS.

## NOTABLE MARKINGS

1. **Serial Number:** 489,731 (top center)
2. **Large Red Seal:** Chinese Republic official seal (center bottom) - features traditional Chinese characters in red with ornamental square border
3. **Vignettes:** 
   - Central pastoral scene with figures
   - Multiple small vignettes in corners showing industrial/commercial scenes
   - Allegorical figures representing industry and commerce

4. **Signatures:** 
   - Bottom left: signature of **Hu Weide (胡惟德)** - Chinese Minister
   - Bottom right: signature labeled "Bouveret"
   - Additional Chinese seal stamp (red) with characters

5. **Text stamp/seal:** Circular official stamp in upper right area

6. **Ornamental borders:** Elaborate peach-colored decorative frame throughout with geometric and floral patterns

7. **Languages marked:** English, German, French, Russian (Cyrillic), and Japanese text sections clearly demarcated

**Note:** This is a bearer bond document issued during the early Chinese Republic period, representing debt obligations in multiple currencies and intended for international circulation through major European and Asian financial centers.
