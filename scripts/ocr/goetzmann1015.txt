# Tyler Building Bond Certificate - Transcription

**No. M 1259**

**UNITED STATES OF AMERICA**

**Share or Part $1,000**

---

State of New York, City of New York

## TYLER BUILDING

**Nineteen John Street Corporation**

Certificate Representing a Share or Part in Nineteen John Street Corporation's

**FIRST MORTGAGE SIX PER CENT SINKING FUND GOLD LOAN**

Secured by the premises situated at 17 to 23 John Street in the Borough of Manhattan, City and State of New York.

---

THIS IS TO CERTIFY, that the bearer, hereof, or if this certificate be registered, the registered holder hereof, is the owner of a share or part of the principal sum of

**ONE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($1,000)**

In certain bonds and mortgages of or assumed by Nineteen John Street Corporation, a corporation existing under and by virtue of the laws of the State of New York (hereinafter called the "Mortgagor"), for an aggregate principal amount of Four Million Four Hundred Thousand Dollars ($4,400,000), executed and delivered to The New York Trust Company, as Trustee (hereinafter called the "Trustee"), and secured by a mortgage dated as of October 1, 1925, of the real property known as the Tyler Building, given to secure the Nineteen John Street Corporation's First Mortgage Six Per Cent Sinking Fund Gold Loan, bearing date as of October 1, 1925, and bearing interest from October 1, 1925.

The said bonds and mortgages are payable in gold coin of the United States of America of the present standard of weight and fineness, or in gold certificates, legal tender for gold coin, with accrued interest thereon, at the principal office of the Trustee in the City of New York.

This certificate shall be payable (unless the same shall have been called for redemption) in gold coin of the United States of America or in gold certificates as aforesaid, at the principal office of the Trustee in the City of New York, on the surrender thereof duly endorsed on the back hereof, and upon the surrender of the interest warrants hereto attached as they severally mature.

Both the principal amount herein expressed and the interest in respect thereof shall be payable at the principal office of the Trustee in the City of New York, provided that, at the option of the Trustee, payment of interest may be made by transfer to any banking house in the City of New York.
