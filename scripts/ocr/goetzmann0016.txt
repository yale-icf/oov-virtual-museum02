Toe den ontstang en intezeit deugels-
den sullen de Heeren Commissarissen
daeren / des dingsdaegs, woenssdaegs,
vrydaegs en satersdaegs 's morgens, han
negen tot elf ueren; en tot het ontstah-
gen uer Bank of alschriftbriefjes, zul-
len de Bockhouwders/ alle dagen/ buiten
Zondagen/ ueeeri zijn/ Des
morgens han tien tot twaelf ueren.

Tweemael des Iaers/ namentlijk
in Januarij en Julij/ sullen de boechen ne-

**English Translation:**

7.
At the opening and closing of the windows, the gentlemen Commissioners shall be there / on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays and Saturdays' mornings, from nine to eleven hours; and at the opening of the Bank or all letters of exchange, the Bookkeepers shall be there all days, except Sundays, until nine o'clock. From ten to twelve hours in the morning.

8.
He who has entered something in the morning shall have it settled / on that same day still be assured if it is, shall be able to have it, in the afternoon from three to four hours; provided that he pays six stuivers therefor. And he who on that time still wished to withdraw / shall be enrolled, provided also that every party pays six stuivers.

Twice a year, namely in January and July, the books shall be—
