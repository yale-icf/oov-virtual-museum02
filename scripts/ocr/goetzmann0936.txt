# Transcription

**Original (French):**

N° 2100

CHAMBRE IMPERIALE ET ROYALE D'ASSURANCE D'ANVERS

[Central circular emblem with ship imagery and text "NEPTVNUS LEFDNDI"]

AU NOM DE DIEU.

Nous soussignés Directeurs de la Chambre d'Affairance d'Anvers, reconnaissons & confessons avoir pris aux Risques, Perils & Fortunes de la Chambre de Vous _____ [handwritten: le Sieur Iuillet Iuillet ADI] pour le compte mesure de qu'il appartient à la Somme ci-après mentionnée sur

[Handwritten text - partially illegible due to cursive script and age]

[Multiple paragraphs of printed text describing insurance terms and conditions, with some handwritten insertions]

[Bottom section contains additional printed text regarding maritime insurance procedures and claims]

Signatures at bottom:
[Handwritten signatures]
Thomas Joseph De Bye
[Additional signature - unclear]

---

**English Translation:**

N° 2100

IMPERIAL AND ROYAL INSURANCE CHAMBER OF ANTWERP

IN THE NAME OF GOD.

We undersigned Directors of the Insurance Chamber of Antwerp, acknowledge and confess that we have taken at the Risks, Perils & Fortunes of the Chamber from You _____ [handwritten: the Sieur Iuillet Iuillet ADI] for the account of whom it may concern the Sum hereinafter mentioned...

[Continues with standard insurance policy language regarding maritime risks, merchant goods, ship conditions, cargo coverage, and claims procedures]

---

**Notable markings:**

- Document number: N° 2100 (top left)
- Date notation: 31 October 1774 [per document description]
- Central circular seal/emblem with ship imagery
- Multiple handwritten insertions and annotations throughout
- Two signatures at bottom (partially legible: "Thomas Joseph De Bye" and another)
- Left margin contains vertical printed text (difficult to read in full due to angle and quality)
- Wax seal impression visible (lower left)
