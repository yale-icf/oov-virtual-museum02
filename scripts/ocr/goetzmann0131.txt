# Transcription

**Original (Dutch):**

Pasquins
WINDKAART
op de
WINDNEGOTIE
Van't Jaar 1720

Prins Fredirks mantel is voor al
de luws-gezinden,
Nu de cen'tge schulplaats om hun
verlujheid te vinden.

**English translation:**

Pasquin's
WIND CHART
on the
WIND TRADE
of the Year 1720

Prince Frederik's mantle is above all
for the lukewarm-minded,
Now the only hiding place for their
idleness to be found.

---

**Notable markings:**

- Decorative border frame with engraved line work
- Four allegorical figures surrounding a central banner/scroll (two at top, two at bottom)
- Figure at top center wearing a conical hat
- Engraved in classical satirical style typical of early 18th-century Dutch political commentary
- No visible signatures or official stamps, but bears characteristics of a printed satirical broadsheet or playing card from "Het Groote Tafereel" (The Great Theatre)
