# Transcription

Know all men by these presents, that we, Nathan H. Ballon & Mccoy D. Walmores & Deo E. Bostick, Jas W. Cleaph

are held and firmly bound unto Res. a. Farnell & a. Sauncer, Tably. P. W. & Nathaniel Atcherwerds, Jas S. Hargrood Lowry, Jassella Starges Elyan Thos, Jas Millers, Elyard Mary & M'Leay,

in the just and full sum of Nineteen hundred dollars

dollars, for the payment of which, well and truly to be made, we and each of us, bind ourselves, our heirs, executors and administrators, jointly and severally, firmly, by these presents, sealed with our seals, and dated this 10th day of April 1852

The condition of the above obligation is such, that whereas the above bound

N. Ballon

this day filed a Bill of complaint in the Clerk's office of the Court of Chancery, at Jackson, for the district composed of the county of Madison, for the Western Division of the State of Tennessee, against said

Thos Thos Farnell and others)

and prayed for and obtained an Injunction upon the matters set forth in said Bill. Now should the said,

Haltoone

prosecute his suit on said Bill with effect, or in case he shall fail therein, shall pay to the said

Leaph,

all such costs and charges as may accrue in consequence thereof, and stand to, abide by and perform such order, judgment or decree, as may be made by said Court, touching said Injunction; then the above obligation to be void; otherwise, to remain in full force and virtue.

[Handwritten notes at bottom:]
Security for Bond this [illegible]
Slave [illegible] or 52 yales 900. D.N.
Hiren age 41, value 900 D.
Accepted by Court John E. Bostick Ced

---

**Notable markings:**

- Handwritten annotations throughout in cursive script
- Signatures of Nathan H. Ballon and John E. Bostick
- Court approval notation at bottom
- Property valuation notes indicating security ($900+)
- Ages noted: 50 and 41 years
- Dated: 10th day of April 1852
- Handwritten note in upper left corner: "Two Slaves or Bond 1900"
