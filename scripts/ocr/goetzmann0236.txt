# Transcription

Anno Regni
GEORGII
REGIS
*Magna Britanniæ, Francia, & Hibernia,*
OCTAVO.

At the Parliament Begun and Holden at *Westminster,*
the Seventeenth Day of *March, Anno Dom.* 1714.
In the First Year of the Reign of our Sovereign
Lord GEORGE, by the Grace of God, of *Great
Britain, France,* and *Ireland,* King, Defender of
the Faith, *&c.*

And from thence Continued by several Prorogations to the Nineteenth Day of
*Odober,* 1721. Being the Eighth Session of this present Parliament.

G                                    R

[Royal coat of arms with lion and unicorn supporters, crown above, shield in center with royal quarterings, and banner below reading "DIEU ET MON DROIT"]

LONDON;

Printed by *John Baskett,* Printer to the Kings most Excellent Majesty, And by the Assigns of *Thomas Newcomb,*
and *Henry Hills, deceas'd.* 1722.

---

## Notable markings:

- **Royal coat of arms** prominently displayed in center of page (lion and unicorn supporters, crown, royal shield with quarterings)
- **Banner inscription**: "DIEU ET MON DROIT"
- **Letters flanking coat of arms**: "G" and "R" (Georgius Rex)
- Document is printed on aged parchment/paper with visible wear and foxing spots
