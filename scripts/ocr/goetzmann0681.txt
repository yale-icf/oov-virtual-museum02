# Transcription

**REPUBLIC OF TEXAS**

**Certificate of Stock in the ten per cent consolidated fund**

**CREATED BY ACT**

**OF FEB. 5TH 1840**

---

**Willing the Republic of Texas to Edward Hall**

**Five Hundred Dollars** Payable in order with interest at the rate of ten per cent per annum, payable semi-annually on the 1st days of June and Dece., at the Treasury Department, in Gold or Silver, on presentation of this Certificate and delivery of the several interest coupons on the margin hereof, the first payment of interest [shall] be made on the 15 days of Dece. 1840, in [further] interest at the pleasure of the Republic after the 1st day of June 1841. Payable interest to be transferred by such Government payment Department. Austin 15 June 1840.

[Signature] James E. Ewan, Comptroller    [Signature] Charles Sewall [unclear] Comm.

*Sanderson & Peck, N.Y. Engs*             *Endicott & Co. New Orleans*

---

**INTEREST COUPONS (10 instances):**

**1,850 Certificate [No.]**

**Notable markings:**

- Engraved allegorical vignettes: left side features a classical figure in circular medallion; center depicts two figures with a ship in background
- Multiple "500" denominations printed in corners
- Ornamental borders and scrollwork throughout
- Ten interest coupons attached at bottom, each with signature marks
- Engravers noted: Sanderson & Peck, N.Y. and Endicott & Co., New Orleans
- Date: June 15, 1840 (or 1841)
