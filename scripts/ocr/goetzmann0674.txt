## English Translation (Summary of Key Content):

**HEADER:**
1914 Year

Russian 4½% railway bonds, guaranteed by the IMPERIAL Russian Government. Unified issue of 1914, consisting of 1,330,000 bonds at 187 rubles 50 kopecks (1 ruble = 1/15 of an imperial [unit]) = 500 francs = 19 pounds 15 shillings 6 pence sterling = 404 German imperial marks = 239 Dutch guilders.

**MAIN TITLE:**
BOND

SOCIETY OF THE
MOSCOW-KIEV-VORONEZH
RAILWAY

at 187 rubles 50 kopecks (1 ruble = 1/15 imperial) = 500 francs = 19.15.6 pounds sterling = 404 German imperial marks = 239 Dutch guilders.

TO BEARER,

[Conditions text describes: The bond represents a fractional share in the railway's 4.5% guaranteed bonds. Interest payments (coupons) are payable twice yearly. The bonds were issued under government guarantee. Detailed provisions regarding transfer, loss, and redemption of bonds are included. Various Russian and European banks are listed as authorized payment agents.]

**FRENCH SECTION SUMMARY:**
Combined 4½% Russian railway bonds guaranteed by the IMPERIAL Government.
Series 1914, composed of 1,330,000 bonds
of 187.50 Rubles (1 ruble = 1/15 imperial) = 500 Francs = £19.15.6 = Mark 404 = Fl. P.-B. 239.

BOND

OF THE MOSCOW-KIEV-VORONEZH RAILWAY COMPANY

[Similar conditions and terms as the Russian version]

Moscow, 1914.

## Notable Markings:

- **Serial Number:** №0567359 (appears twice, top and bottom)
- **Seals:** Two ornate circular Imperial Russian seals (eagles) in top corners and additional seal (marked "М.Ф." - Ministry of Finance) in top right
- **Signatures:** Four signatures visible in Russian section with official titles (Board members and Imperial Government representative)
- **Decorative Border:** Ornate geometric and wave patterns frame the entire document
- **Bilingual Format:** Document presented in parallel Russian (Cyrillic) and French (Latin script)
- **Date:** Moscow, 1914
- **Language Notes:** Text uses pre-revolutionary Russian orthography (including characters like ъ and ѐ that were eliminated in the 1918 spelling reform)
