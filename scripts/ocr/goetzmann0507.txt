PRICE-CURRENT OF SECURITIES. Year 1822. Amsterdam, Thursday, 16 May. No. 39.

A printed Amsterdam stock-exchange price list quoting bid-and-ask ranges for three trading days (14, 15 and 16 May); the 16th fell on a holiday (Feestdag), so only the 14th and 15th are priced. The sheet is arranged by issuer and by country.

United Netherlands — the active and deferred national debt, syndicate bonds, and Netherlands bank-notes; the loans of the Treasury of the City of Amsterdam (negotiations of 1791–1819, redeemable, some payable in bank-money or cash); and Middelburg obligations.

Domestic private negotiations — shares and certificates in Dutch funds and infrastructure through named banking houses (van Harpen & Pluym & Backer; Mercker & van Ophoven; Ketwich & Voombergh), including the Rhineland negotiation, the Muidersluis, the Amstelveen road, the Slooten causeway, the ceded East-Frisian domains, the Society for Beneficence (Vlaer & Kol, Utrecht), the Nieuwe Diep & Koegras and Groote Wegen works, the Linge–Steenenhoek canal, and shipping and lands in Overyssel.

Foreign negotiations:
- America — United States federal debt placed through the Amsterdam houses of Van Staphorst (3% and 6% funds, 1791 issues), Daniel Crommelin & Sons (deferred and "liquidated debts"), Texier, Angely & Massac, Croese & Co., and Stadnitski & van Heukelom (certificates of liquidated debts and obligations on American lands).
- Russia — Hope & Co. obligations, inscriptions in the Grand Book (in paper and in silver), and Hamburg administration certificates.
- Denmark — toll, crown, loan-and-exchange-bank, and Asiatic Company obligations, and the National Bank.
- Austria — Vienna Bank obligations and metalliques through Goll & Co., Hope & Co., and J. Osy & Son; Vienna Bank shares; the 1820 loan and the 37½-million loan.
- Italy / Sicily — Milan monte certificates (Saportas); Neapolitan consolidated debt (Lamaison & Bouwer).
- Spain — obligations via Echenique & Co. and Hope & Co.; the 1820 Paris loan; premium-bills; vales reales.
- Great Britain — India and Bank stock, South Sea annuities, and 3% and 4% Consols (settlement 12 Aug. 1822).
- France — inscriptions in the Grand Book deliverable at Paris, liquidation acknowledgements, and the City of Paris premium loan (bank shares via Buys & Kerkhoven).
- Lordships of Count Bentinck, the Three English Princes, the Grand Duchy of Baden (Goll & Sons, Frankfurt), and Colombia (London obligations).

Exchange and specie rates of Amsterdam (15 May) — bill rates on Madrid, Bilbao, Cadiz, Seville, Lisbon, Oporto, Naples, Venice, Livorno, Genoa, Paris, Bordeaux, London, Hamburg, Vienna, Augsburg, Frankfurt, Berlin and St. Petersburg, followed by quotations for gold coin (guineas, sovereigns, louis-d'or, bar gold) and silver coin (Mexican dollars, French and Brabant crowns, five-franc pieces, rixdollars).
