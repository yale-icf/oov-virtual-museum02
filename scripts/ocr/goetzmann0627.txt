# Document Transcription

**Original (French):**

N° 391                                    DENIER 20. Mars 1698

JE JEAN DE TURMENYES, Conseiller du Roy en fes Confeils, Garde de fon Trefor Royal, Confeile avoir receu comptant en cette Ville de Paris, de Jean Eloy Udtin [argentier de Monsieur l'Archevêque de Rouen]

[several lines with handwritten corrections and annotations]

la Somme de huil mille livres en Louis d'or, d'argent, & autre Monnoye pour le principal de quatre cens livres de Rente qu'il ly feront vendus & constituez par Messieurs; les Prevost des Marchands & Efchevins de ladite Ville de Paris fur le Million de livres auquels & effectifs de rente annuelle & perpetuelle à eux nouvellemnt alienez par Sa Majefté en confequence de fon Edit du mois de Mars 1698. regiftré où befoin a été, à prendre fur les droits d'Aydes & Gabelles de France, pour jouïr par ledit Udtin à raifon de Denier Vingt, ainfi qu'il fera plus au long expliqué par le Contrat de confti tution qui luy fera expedié de ladite Rente par lesdits Sieurs Prevost des Marchands & Efchevins, en execution dudit Edit. De laquelle Somme de huit mille livres

à moy ordonnée pour employer au fait de ma Charge, je quitte lesdits Sieurs & tous autres. A Paris le [date]

jour d'[illegible] mil fix cent quatre-vingt-dix-huit.

Quittance du Garde du Trésor Royal, Année mil fix cent quatre-vingt-dix-huit.

[Signature: de Turmenyes]

M. Sougis                N.e

---

**English Translation:**

I, JEAN DE TURMENYES, Counselor to the King in his Councils, Keeper of his Royal Treasury, certify that I have received in cash in this City of Paris, from Jean Eloy Udtin [financial agent of the Archbishop of Rouen]

the Sum of eight thousand livres in gold louis, silver, and other coin for the principal of four hundred livres of annual perpetual rente which will be sold and constituted to him by the Messieurs Provost of Merchants and Aldermen of the said City of Paris on the Million livres of annual and perpetual rente newly alienated to them by His Majesty in consequence of his Edit of the month of March 1698, registered where necessary has been, to be drawn from the rights of Aides and Gabelles of France, for the enjoyment by the said Udtin at the rate of Denier Vingt, as will be more fully explained by the Contract of constitution which will be issued to him for said Rente by the said Messieurs Provost of Merchants and Aldermen, in execution of said Edit. From which Sum of eight thousand livres

ordered to me for employment in the discharge of my office, I acquit the said Messieurs and all others. At Paris on [date]

day of [illegible] thousand six hundred ninety-eight.

Receipt of the Keeper of the Royal Treasury, Year thousand six hundred ninety-eight.
