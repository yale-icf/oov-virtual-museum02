## Main Document Text

**REPUBLICA DE CUBA**

Bono de la Deuda Exterior Pagadero en Oro en 1949

Four and One-half Per Cent. Gold Bond, Due 1949 [External Loan]

The Republic of Cuba, for value received, promises to pay to the bearer hereof on the first day of August, 1949, in the City of New York, United States of America, Five Hundred Thousand Dollars, in gold coin of the United States of America of the present standard of weight and fineness, or, at the holder's option, in the City of London, England, £2 880 13s. 2d. Sterling, or in Frankfurt a.M. or Berlin, Germany, 2,100,000 German Marks, or in Paris, France, 2,590,000 Francs, and to pay interest on the above named sums, in any of said cities in the said respective currencies, at the rate of four and one-half per centum per annum from the first day of August, 1909, semi-annually on the first days of February and August in each year upon presentation of this bond for endorsement of statement of payment hereon.

The amount represented by this temporary bond forms a part of the loan of sixteen million, five hundred thousand dollars ($16,500,000) in gold coin of the United States of America, or its equivalent in foreign moneys as above stated, issued in pursuance of the decree dated January 25, 1909, of the Provisional Governor of Cuba and

in pursuance of the agreement between the Republic of Cuba and Speyer & Co., dated August__________, 1909, which decree together with extracts from said agreement is printed on the back hereof.

The principal, as well as the interest of this bond is forever exempt from all kinds of Cuban taxes, stamps, duties or assessments (whether of the Republic of Cuba or of any province or municipality therein) which exist or may be imposed hereafter. The Republic of Cuba will provide a sinking-fund for the retirement of said bonds as prescribed in said agreement with Speyer & Co. This bond is subject to be drawn for redemption at par and interest as stated in Article VI. of said agreement, and is also subject to redemption, at the option of the Republic of Cuba, at one hundred and five per cent. and accrued interest, as stated in Article VIII. of said agreement. At the date fixed for the redemption of this bond the interest thereon shall cease.

This bond shall not be valid or obligatory until the same shall have been duly authenticated by the execution by Speyer & Co. of the certificate endorsed hereon.

**HAVANA, CUBA, August 2, 1909.**
