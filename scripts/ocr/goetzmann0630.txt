# Transcription

## Original Text (Dutch/French Bilingual):

**LEFT SIDE (DUTCH):**

CERTIFICAAT
Van 6 per Cents
RUSSISCHE FONDSEN
IN
Bank-Assignatien.

No. 3074

HOUDER dezes is geregtigd tot eene Inschrijving van DUIZEND ROEBELS 6 per Cents Russische Fondsen, betaalbaar in BANK-ASSIGNATIEN, ingeschreven in het Streef-Boek te St. Petersburg, ten name van het ADMINISTRATIE-KANTOOR te Amsterdam, onder directie van

HOPE en COMP.,
KETWICH en VOOMBERGH,
EN
WED. W. BORSKI.

Waarvan de Interessen voor rekening en risico van den HOUDER ontvangen en uitbetaald zullen worden op Couvert, ingevolge Art. 6 van het hierboven gestelde Reglement. De HOUDER van dit Certificaat zal ten allen tijde geregtigd zijn de originele INSCRIPTIE terug te vorderen, tegen teruggeving van dit Certificaat, met de onverschenen Coupons, en het daarbij afgegeven Bewijs voor nieuwe COUPONS, en tegen betaling van de ontvangen Interessen volgens Art. 10 van het Reglement.

Amsterdam, den 7 September 1827.

Gezien bij mij
[Signature] Joannes Brouwgh
Notarius

Hierbij afgegeven Tien Halfjaarige COUPONS, tot is Juli 1829.

## English Translation:

**LEFT SIDE (DUTCH):**

CERTIFICATE
Of 6 Per Cents
RUSSIAN FUNDS
IN
Bank Assignations.

No. 3074

THE HOLDER of this certificate is entitled to an Inscription of ONE THOUSAND ROUBLES 6 per Cents Russian Funds, payable in BANK ASSIGNATIONS, inscribed in the Grand-Book at St. Petersburg, in the name of the ADMINISTRATION OFFICE in Amsterdam, under the direction of

HOPE and COMP.,
KETWICH and VOOMBERGH,
AND
WED. W. BORSKI.

Of which the Interest shall be received for the account and risk of the HOLDER and paid against the return of the Couvert, according to Art. 6 of the Regulation stated above. The HOLDER of this Certificate shall at all times be entitled to claim the original INSCRIPTION back, against return of this Certificate, with the un-cashed Coupons, and the Evidence given with it for new COUPONS, and against payment of the Interests received according to Art. 10 of the Regulation.

Amsterdam, the 7th September 1827.

Seen by me
[Signature] Joannes Brouwgh
Notary

Herewith issued Ten Semi-annual COUPONS, until July 1829.

---

**RIGHT SIDE (FRENCH):**

CERTIFICATE
OF RUSSIAN INSCRIPTION
at 6 Per Cent
IN
Bank Assignations.

No. 4000:—

THE BEARER is entitled to an Inscription of ONE THOUSAND ROUBLES capital at 6 per Cent, of the Public Debt of Russia, payable in BANK ASSIGNATIONS, inscribed in the Grand-Book at St. Petersburg, in the name of the ADMINISTRATION BUREAU, established in Amsterdam, under the direction of

HOPE and COMP.,
KETWICH and VOOMBERGH,
AND
Widow W. BORSKI.

Of which the Interest shall be received for the account and risk of the BEARER and paid against the return of the Coupons, according to Art. 6 of the Regulation. The BEARER of this Certificate may at any time claim the original INSCRIPTION, against restitution of the Certificate, with the non-matured Coupons belonging thereto, and the ACKNOWLEDGMENT attached thereto to obtain new COUPONS, by means of payment of the Interests mentioned in Art. 10 of the Regulation.

Amsterdam, the 7th September 1827.

[Signatures] Widow W. Borski
M. W. Borskei

Delivered Ten COUPONS of Semi-annual Rent, until July 1829.

Signed by     Signed by
[Signature]

## Notable Markings:

- **Certificate Number:** No. 3074 (Dutch side); No. 4000 (French side)
- **Principal Amount:** 1,000 Roubles
- **Interest Rate:** 6 per cent
- **Issue Date:** Amsterdam, 7 September 1827
- **Signatures:** 
  - Dutch side: Joannes Brouwgh (Notarius)
  - French side: Veuve W. Borski, M. W. Borskei
  - Bottom signature: [illegible but present]
- **Coupons:** Ten semi-annual coupons issued, valid until July 1829
- **Ornamental Border:** Decorative Dutch/Flemish typographic border frame
- **Format:** Bilingual Dutch-French document typical of Amsterdam banking houses in the 19th century serving both Dutch and French-speaking investors
