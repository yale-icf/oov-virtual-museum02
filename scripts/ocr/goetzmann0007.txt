**English Translation:**

(4.)

26. The said buyers must satisfy [the requirements] not at 30 guilders, or 100 if the price shall be at the purchase of the Regents.

27. The sellers of Shares must alone be bound to the Company, as they are not [liable] on the full Capital, until such time as it is somewhat like its repayment.

28. For those who have laid down their Shares, the Company shall be able to resell them, or the Shares take back for the money, for which they were laid down.

29. If the Company sets its Shares at 50 guilders, being 500 buyers found for a Fund of 4 thousand per 100 guilders, it is to be thought that there will also be buyers [to] come, as there will be a genuine Fund.

30. The South Sea Company in England, although [it has] 500 sufficient funds, [it] also itself employs this means of Shares being sold, of Shares being resold.

31. There the Shares are increased for sale; here may the Shares for the sum of twelve millions; 100 as they now are, [each] one and each per rata his Shares 300 much will be increased, just as this was proposed.

32. It is fitting, the Shares in such manner would be brought to a second Capital, so that what was before, would the Company that receive at 60. and 1000 further. And then an apparent Acre, which has fifty days' guilders, and after merely in Capital is hundred guilders, before she [the Company] was diminished, so all effectively the Capital in all cases, was in the much spiritual.

33. For those already [who] have laid [Shares] of particulars, the diminution could be arranged as clearly; but those of the Company will buy, this shall not be able to be diminished, before the Company has been made full with it.

34. Those whose Shares would hold full, could do that, provided [they] supply in money; which they would be diminished.

35. In this way the Company would have obtained a good Fund, and not unbelievably; that without any addition (such as the Share-part of it) the whole Capital could be made full: and [continues on next page]
