# IMPERIAL CHINESE GOVERNMENT GOLD LOAN OF 1908

## English Text (Left Side):

**IMPERIAL CHINESE GOVERNMENT**

**GOLD LOAN** **OF 1908**

**£5,000,000 STERLING**

**BOND No.** B106822 **OBLIGATION No.** B106822

**IMPERIAL CHINESE GOVERNMENT EMPRUNT IMPERIAL CHINOIS.**
**GOLD LOAN-1908-£5,000,000.** **OR 1908-£5,000,000.**

[Extended terms and conditions text - partially legible]:

"AUTHORIZED BY IMPERIAL EDICT OF OCTOBER 1908..."

"UNCONDITIONALLY GUARANTEED BY THE IMPERIAL CHINESE GOVERNMENT..."

"BOND FOR £20 STERLING"

[Lengthy bond terms and conditions detailing interest payments, principal repayment, and guarantees - text becomes increasingly difficult to read clearly in the smaller print]

## French Text (Right Side):

**OBLIGATION DE £20 STERLING**

[French version of equivalent terms and conditions]

"Un Lot de cent de Ministre de Chine à Paris"

## Notable Markings:

- **Serial Number:** B106822
- **Date:** Paris, 15 Mars, 1908 (March 15, 1908)
- **Denomination:** £20 Sterling (shown in corners)
- **Issuing Bank:** Banque de l'Indo-Chine (footer notation: "POUR LA BANQUE DE L'INDO-CHINE")
- **Printers:** Waterlow & Sons Limited (bottom left); London Wall, London, E.C. (bottom right)
- **Red Seal/Stamp:** Chinese character seal in center of document
- **Signatures:** Two signature lines at bottom (Paris, 15 Mars 1908)
- **Ornate decorative border:** Green Art Nouveau-style design with scrollwork
- **Vignette:** Central illustration showing Chinese harbor with buildings, pagodas, and bridge
